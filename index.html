about

I study Computer Science and Mathematics at MIT. At CSAIL's ALFA Lab, I'm researching a question that's fairly common with LLMs: are the connections it's drawing real, or does the model just want it to be there? Specifically, I look at cases where an LLM hallucinates a link between a CVE and an attack technique it has nothing to do with. At Far Eastern International Bank in Taipei, I'm building a RAG pipeline to help automate compliance due diligence — pulling together the pieces of a transaction that make a case for (or against) flagging it.

I'm looking at software engineering, AI/ML, and hardware-adjacent roles for next year.

Outside of tech, I like early morning long runs, afternoon tennis sessions on hot sunny days, and a good matcha (or coffee) at an aesthetic cafes. if you can't find me, look for the nearest cafe: i'll probably be there :)

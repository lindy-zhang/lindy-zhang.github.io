Flash

The gentle trickle of a stream catches my eye. A droplet lands on my pink sequined Sketchers and another wets my face with a cooling breeze.

My shoes are pink.

My dress is pink.

My face is pink with joy.

"Strike a pose!" my mother shouts from behind. I grin. My favorite phrase has just been said. With the widest smile I can muster (I only smile without my teeth), I place my arms in an all-too-familiar stance and glance at the camera.

Flash. The photo is done.

Flash. The dress is too tight and no longer pink, and the shoes are three sizes too small.

Flash. The smile disappears and is replaced by a frown. Lines that resemble wrinkles frame my forehead.

Flash. The photo is gone.
